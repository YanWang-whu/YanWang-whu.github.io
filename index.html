Selected Research

Background

This project investigates how the neuropeptide INS-1 regulates alcohol perception under high-salt conditions in C. elegans, aiming to understand how neuromodulatory signals reshape behavioral responses under environmental stress.

Research

Through behavioral assays, we examined ethanol chemotaxis under different salt conditions and identified a reversal of avoidance behavior in ins-1 mutants.

We constructed neuron-specific knockout and rescue strains to localize the functional site of INS-1 within the neural circuit.

Calcium imaging experiments were performed to analyze neuronal activity changes and investigate how INS-1 modulates circuit-level responses.

My Participation

I independently designed and conducted behavioral assays and participated in strain construction and validation.

I performed calcium imaging experiments, image processing, and data analysis, and identified ASEL as a key neuron involved in INS-1 signaling.
