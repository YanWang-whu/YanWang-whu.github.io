About

I am an undergraduate student in Biological Sciences at Wuhan University, enrolled in the Hongyi Honors Program. My research interests lie at the intersection of neuroscience, synthetic biology, and induced hypometabolic states (human hibernation). I am particularly interested in understanding how biological systems can be engineered or regulated to achieve novel physiological states.

Research Experience

Yu-Jin Lab, Wuhan University

Investigated neural mechanisms underlying behavioral regulation in C. elegans, focusing on how the neuropeptide ins-1 modulates alcohol chemotaxis under high-salt conditions.

iGEM Project – LOGIC

Designed a multicellular biocomputation system using quorum sensing-based genetic circuits. Developed high-pass and band-pass biological logic gates and implemented spatially distributed computation on agar plates.
